1. Introduction & Background 
 
Indian agriculture depends heavily on the timely availability of water, and the gap between crop 
water demand and actual water supply is one of the biggest drivers of yield loss, farmer distress 
and inefficient canal-water usage. At the same time, national missions such as PMKSY, the 
Digital Agriculture Mission, NMSA and PMFBY are increasingly looking for scalable, satellite-
driven intelligence rather than slow, manual field surveys. 
Earth observation has matured to a point where optical sensors (LISS-III, AWiFS, Sentinel-2, 
Landsat, MODIS) and microwave SAR sensors (EOS-04, Sentinel-1, and the upcoming NISAR 
mission) can be fused to deliver continuous, all-weather monitoring — something that is critical 
during the cloud-heavy kharif/monsoon season when optical data alone becomes unusable for 
weeks at a stretch. 
This project proposes an automated, AI-driven pipeline that goes beyond simple vegetation-
index monitoring. It identifies crop type, tracks moisture stress in a phenology-aware (growth-
stage-specific) manner, and converts that stress signal into a practical, field-level irrigation 
advisory — closing the loop from raw satellite pixels to an action a farmer or canal-command 
operator can actually use. 
2. Problem Statement 
 
Despite the availability of rich satellite archives, several practical gaps remain in how moisture 
stress and irrigation needs are currently assessed: 
• 
Cloud cover during the kharif/monsoon season frequently blocks optical satellites for 
weeks, leaving large monitoring gaps when crops are most vulnerable to water stress. 
• 
Most existing stress-detection approaches rely on simple NDVI thresholds and ignore 
the fact that the same index value means something different at sowing, tillering, 
flowering or maturity — i.e. they are not phenology-aware. 
• 
Crop-type maps are often produced late in the season or require extensive, costly 
ground surveys, limiting their use for early-season planning. 
• 
Irrigation scheduling on the ground is frequently based on convention or guesswork 
rather than a quantified 8-day water deficit, leading to either water wastage or avoidable 
crop stress. 
• 
Crop insurance verification under PMFBY lacks a continuous, auditable, satellite-backed 
evidence trail of stress and damage progression. 
This project addresses each of these gaps within a single, fusion-based, end-to-end pipeline.

4. Unique Selling Points (USP) 
 
Several teams at any geospatial hackathon will build an NDVI-threshold stress map. The points 
below are what are intended to differentiate this submission — each one targets a real adoption 
barrier in operational agriculture monitoring, not just a modelling trick. 
4.1 Foundation-Model-Powered Few-Shot Crop Classification 
Instead of relying purely on hand-engineered spectral indices and large labelled datasets, the 
pipeline plugs in pretrained Earth-observation foundation model embeddings (e.g. Google 
DeepMind AlphaEarth Foundations, NASA-IBM Prithvi-EO-2.0, or the Clay Foundation Model). 
These embeddings already encode global land-cover and crop-phenology structure, so a 
lightweight classifier on top can reach strong accuracy with far fewer ground-truth points — 
directly solving the “ground truth is scarce” problem that limits most operational crop maps. 
4.2 True Stage-Level Optical–SAR Fusion, Not Just Gap-Filling 
Most fusion pipelines use SAR only to patch holes left by clouds. This design fuses VV/VH 
backscatter and texture with optical indices at the level of each phenological stage (sowing, 
vegetative, flowering, maturity), so the stress index itself is stage-aware rather than a single 
blended number — giving agronomically meaningful, not just statistically smoothed, output. 
4.3 Confidence-Aware Advisory Engine 
Every irrigation advisory ships with an uncertainty flag (via Monte Carlo dropout / quantile 
estimation), so a canal operator or farmer can see when the model is confident versus when 
ground verification is recommended. This single feature is what separates a research demo 
from something an operator would actually trust enough to act on. 
4.4 Farmer-First Multilingual Delivery Layer 
A dashboard alone rarely reaches the field. An LLM-based natural-language layer converts the 
numeric deficit/advisory output into short, plain-language SMS, WhatsApp or IVR voice 
messages in regional languages (Hindi and others), closing the last-mile gap between a 
geospatial model and an actual farmer decision. 
4.5 Canal Command Water-Budget Optimizer 
Beyond visualizing deficit, the system aggregates pixel-level demand up to the canal outlet / 
command-area level and suggests a relative water-release prioritization across outlets — 
turning a monitoring map into an operational scheduling input for irrigation departments. 
4.6 NISAR-Ready, Future-Proof SAR Ingestion 
The SAR ingestion module is built as a swappable adapter so that once NISAR data streams 
become available, the same pipeline can ingest higher-resolution L-band/S-band backscatter

8. Detailed Methodology 
 
8.1 Data Pre-processing 
• 
Atmospheric correction of optical imagery and cloud/shadow masking. 
• 
Refined-Lee speckle filtering on SAR imagery to suppress noise while preserving edges. 
• 
Temporal compositing at weekly, fortnightly and 8-day intervals to build analysis-ready 
stacks. 
8.2 Feature Extraction 
• 
Vegetation indices: NDVI, EVI, NDWI, VCI. 
• 
SAR features: VV, VH backscatter and the VH/VV ratio. 
• 
Texture features via Grey-Level Co-occurrence Matrix (GLCM). 
• 
Phenological metrics: start of season (SOS), peak growth date, length of growing period 
(LGP). 
8.3 Crop Type Classification 
• 
Generate foundation-model embeddings for each pixel/parcel time series. 
• 
Train Random Forest / XGBoost classifiers on embeddings plus hand-crafted multi-
temporal features. 
• 
Produce a seasonal crop-type map for the pilot command area. 
8.4 Phenology-Aware Moisture Stress Detection 
• 
Compute NDVI anomalies relative to the historical stage-specific baseline.

• 
Combine NDWI, SAR backscatter sensitivity and soil moisture proxies into a Vegetation 
Condition Index (VCI) and Soil Moisture Index (SMI). 
• 
Classify stress severity separately for each growth stage rather than using one global 
threshold. 
8.5 Water Deficit Estimation & Irrigation Advisory 
• 
Estimate 8-day crop water demand (ETc) using the FAO-56 crop-coefficient method (Kc 
× reference ET). 
• 
Quantify the deficit between ETc and the sum of effective rainfall and actual 
evapotranspiration. 
• 
Translate the deficit into a categorical irrigation-status map (no action / monitor / irrigate 
soon / irrigate now), each carrying a confidence flag. 
8.6 AI/ML Model Development 
• 
Combine optical, SAR and meteorological features into a unified feature store. 
• 
Train LSTM / Temporal Fusion Transformer models for temporal stress-progression 
prediction, and U-Net / fine-tuned SAM for spatial parcel segmentation. 
• 
Apply SHAP-based explainability so every prediction can be traced back to its driving 
indices. 
8.7 Validation 
• 
Compare crop-type output against held-out ground-truth points using Overall Accuracy 
and Kappa coefficient. 
• 
Cross-check stress and deficit outputs against any available field reports, weather-
station records or known stress events for logical consistency.

16. Conclusion 
 
This project converts a well-known but operationally difficult challenge — turning raw, multi-
sensor satellite data into a trustworthy, field-actionable irrigation advisory — into a single, 
modular AI pipeline. By fusing optical and SAR data at the phenological-stage level, layering in 
foundation-model embeddings for few-shot crop classification, and closing the loop with a 
confidence-aware, multilingual advisory delivery layer, the solution is designed to be both 
hackathon-demoable in 30 hours and credible as a path toward real operational deployment 
under programmes like PMKSY, the Digital Agriculture Mission and PMFBY. 
Team MeraCodeLikhDo — Gaurav Tiwari, Shubham Singh, Prajjwal Singh and Krishna 
Gupta — aims to demonstrate this end-to-end, from satellite pixel to farmer advisory.
